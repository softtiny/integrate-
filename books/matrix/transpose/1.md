**Matrix transpose** is one of the simplest yet most frequently used operations in linear algebra.

### Definition
The **transpose** of a matrix A (usually written $A^T$) is obtained by **flipping it over its main diagonal** — that is, turning rows into columns and columns into rows.

Formal rule:
If A is a matrix of size **m × n**, then Aᵀ is **n × m** and:

$(A^T)_{ij} = A_{ij}$

### Quick examples

1. $
    A = \begin{bmatrix}
    1 & 2 & 3\\
    4 & 5 & 6 
    \end{bmatrix}\\
    A^T = \begin{bmatrix}
    1 & 4\\
    2 & 5\\ 
    3 & 6
    \end{bmatrix}
    $


2. Column vector → becomes row vector
$V=\begin{bmatrix}
    7\\
    8 \\ 
    9 
\end{bmatrix}\\
V^T=\begin{bmatrix}
    7 & 8 & 9\\
\end{bmatrix}
$


3. Square matrix
    $M = \begin{bmatrix}
    1 & 2 & 3\\
    4 & 5 & 6\\
    7 & 8 & 9\\
    \end{bmatrix}\\
    M^T = \begin{bmatrix}
    1 & 4 & 7\\
    2 & 5 & 8\\
    3 & 6 & 9\\
    \end{bmatrix}$


4. Symmetric matrix (very important case)

    If $M^T = M$ → the matrix is **symmetric**
    Example:
    \[\begin{bmatrix}
    4 & 1 & -2\\
    1 & 3 & 7\\
    -2 & 7 & 11\\
    \end{bmatrix}\]


### Most important properties (cheat sheet)

| Property                          | Formula                            | Comment                                  |
|-------------------------------|--------------------------------------|------------------------------------------|
| Transpose of transpose        | $(A^T)^T = A$                           | double transpose brings back original    |
| Transpose of sum              | $(A+B)^T=A^T+B^T$                | transpose distributes over addition      |
| Transpose of scalar multiple  | $(cA)^T=cA^T$                       | scalars come out                         |
| Transpose of product          | $(AB)^T=B^TA^T$                       | **order reverses** — very important!     |
| Transpose of inverse          | $(A^{-1})^T=(A^T)^{-1}$                     | if A is invertible                       |
| Diagonal elements             | stay the same                        | only off-diagonal elements are swapped   |

### Quick memory trick for $(AB)^T = B^TA^T$
"Transpose of product → **reverse the order** and transpose each factor"
