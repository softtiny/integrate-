In linear algebra, an invertible matrix is a square matrix that admits a multiplicative inverse with respect to matrix multiplication. 

This definition assumes the context of matrices representing linear transformations on finite dimensional vector spaces over a field, ensuring that the algebraic structure supports the necessary operations without singularities arising from division by zero or similar issues in non field rings. 

The inverse of a product of invertible matrices reverses the order of the factors. 

The transpose operation preserves invertibility and interacts compatibly with inversion.

Similarity preserves the property of invertibility.

The sign of the determinant encodes the orientation of the parallelogram relative to the standard basis of the plane. A positive determinant indicates a counterclockwise orientation of the vectors, aligning with the right-hand rule convention, while a negative determinant signifies a clockwise orientation, effectively reflecting the parallelogram across the origin. This signed interpretation distinguishes the determinant from mere area computation, capturing both magnitude and directional sense in the plane.

The sign of the determinant thus determines the orientation of the basis: a positive value indicates the same handedness as the standard basis, while a negative value signals an opposite handedness.

In linear algebra, the rank of a matrix is the dimension of the vector space spanned by its columns.equivalently, the dimension of the space spanned by its rows.

The equality of column rank and row rank is a fundamental theorem: the dimension of the column space always equals the dimension of the row space, allowing a single unambiguous rank value.

The column rank of a matrix is the dimension of its column space, the subspace spanned by its column vectors. Equivalently, it is the maximum number of linearly independent columns.

The row rank is the dimension of the row space, the subspace spanned by its row vectors, or equivalently the maximum number of linearly independent rows.
