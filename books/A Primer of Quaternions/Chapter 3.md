
### Addition of Quaternions

* **Definition of Quaternion Addition:** * Establishing the algebraic meaning of adding two quaternions, $q_1 + q_2$.
    * Treating a quaternion as an operator composed of a scalar part (a pure number) and a vector part (a directed step in space).

* **The Commutative Law:** Demonstrating that the order of addition does not alter the result: $q_1 + q_2 = q_2 + q_1$.
* **The Associative Law:**  Proving that when adding three or more quaternions, the grouping of terms yields the same result: $(q_1 + q_2) + q_3 = q_1 + (q_2 + q_3)$.
* **Closure and Component Addition:** Showing that adding two quaternions results in another quaternion, where the scalar parts are added together as real numbers, and the vector parts are compounded according to the laws of steps (parallelogram law).

### Formulas and Geometric Transformations

* **The Scalar and Vector Decompositions:** Introducing the standard notation for isolating the parts of a quaternion: $S \cdot q$ (the Scalar part) and $V \cdot q$ (the Vector part), such that $q = Sq + Vq$.
* **Multiplication and Expansion Formulas:** Developing formulas for the products of sums and the expansion of complex expressions.
    * Formulating how the product of two pure vectors results in a scalar part (related to the cosine of the angle between them) and a vector part (related to the sine of the angle and perpendicular to their plane).
* **Trigonometric Identifications:** Showing how fundamental formulas of plane and spherical trigonometry naturally unfold from the algebraic expansions of quaternion multiplication and addition.
* **Connections to Semi-Cartesian Geometry:** Bridging quaternion formulas directly into standard coordinates, demonstrating how solid analytical geometry can be executed more fluidly through the algebraic rules established in this chapter.
