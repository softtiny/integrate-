Definition (Hathaway §2): To project step AB onto a line ℓ along direction d, draw parallels to d through A and B; where they meet ℓ are A′ and B′. The projected step is A′B′.

Hathaway's key theorem: proj(AB + BC) = proj(AB) + proj(BC). Toggle the three steps and see how their projections add up.

Scale factor k distributes through projection. The Plagiograph is a mechanical linkage that realises this: it takes an input step and outputs a scaled, direction-preserved copy.

Scale k 1.5×
Angle 25°
Step proportion (§3): proj(k · AB) = k · proj(AB). Scaling a step scales its projection by the same factor. The plagiograph exploits this: it is a parallelogram linkage whose pivot ratio sets k, turning it into a geometric multiplier.
