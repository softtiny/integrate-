# Adjoint of a Matrix

The adjoint (or adjugate) of a matrix is the transpose matrix of the cofactor of the given matrix. For any square matrix A, to calculate its adjoint matrix, we have to first calculate the cofactor matrix of the given matrix and then find its determinant. 


# Transpose of a Matrix

Transpose of a matrix is a new matrix that is obtained by swapping the rows and columns of the given matrix or vice versa

# Example

```math
A = \begin{bmatrix} 2 & 5 & 3 \\ 4 & 7 & 0\end{bmatrix}\\
A^{T} = \begin{bmatrix} 2 & 4 \\ 5 & 7 \\ 3 & 0\end{bmatrix}\\
```

### Determinant of Transpose of a Matrix

The determinant of the transpose of a matrix A is equal to the determinant of A itself
